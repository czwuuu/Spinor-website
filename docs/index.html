What is Spinor?

Spinor is a mathematical tool for describing particle spin, widely used in quantum mechanics and particle physics. It is a mathematical object that has special transformation properties under rotation, which is more fundamental than vectors.

In mathematics, spinor can be regarded as a representation of the special linear group SL(2,C) over the complex field. This representation is particularly important in physics because it is related to the double cover of the Lorentz group.

Applications of Spinor in particle physics

Spinor plays a crucial role in describing fermions, Dirac equations, and the standard model. Through spinor, we can:

  • Describe fermions with spin 1/2
  • Explain spin-orbit coupling in Dirac equations
  • Understand parity violation in weak interactions
  • Build the classification of fundamental particles in the standard model
